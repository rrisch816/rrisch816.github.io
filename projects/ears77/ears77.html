Final Project: Modeling Wildfire Risk across Alaska with Machine Learning

TLDR: The goal of this study is to predictively model wildfire occurrence in Alaska with machine learning (ML). We identified three key factors in wildfire occurrence: land cover type, average summer temperature, and average summer precipitation. To train our ML models, we use historical wildfire polygon data as our ground truth. We decided to experiment with three different types of models: Decision Tree, Logistic Regression, and Random Forest, and compare their performances. All data preparation, and final visualizations, were done in ArcGIS Pro. Lastly, we sourced predicted future summer temperatures and precipitation in Alaska and ran this data through our best performing model, the Random Forest, thereby predicting future areas of Alaska at risk for wildfires.
